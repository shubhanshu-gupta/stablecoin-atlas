1.  Scope Decision: Entity Pages Before Comparison Tool
The growth plan identifies two closely related features: (a) Stablecoin Directory / Entity Pages and (b) a Comparison Tool (“Compare USDC vs USDT vs PYUSD”). Both are valuable. This PRD recommends building Entity Pages first and deferring the Comparison Tool to Phase 3, for four concrete reasons.

1.1  The newsletter has already created a credibility debt

CONTEXT:  “Each stablecoin on the Atlas now links directly to its most recent reserve attestation — alongside the asset type, backing mechanism, and depeg history. If you hold any stablecoin and haven’t looked at its attestation page, this week’s concept section is a good reason to start.”

This copy has already been sent to subscribers. The attestation pages it references do not yet exist. Every day they remain missing is a day a subscriber checks and finds nothing — eroding the trust the newsletter was designed to build. Entity pages fulfil this promise with precision: each stablecoin gets its own page, and that page surfaces the reserve attestation link.

1.2  Entity Pages are a structural prerequisite for the Comparison Tool
The comparison tool requires clean, structured, field-level data for each stablecoin: peg currency, issuer, chain support, regulatory alignment, reserve composition, depeg history. None of that data currently exists in a queryable form. Building the comparison tool before the entity pages means either:
	•	hardcoding comparison data (brittle, doesn’t scale), or
	•	building the entity data model twice (wasted effort).

The correct sequence is: data model → entity pages → comparison tool. This PRD establishes the data model and the pages. The comparison tool can be shipped as a thin layer on top in Phase 3.

1.3  Entity Pages serve all three user personas; the Comparison Tool primarily serves one
The portal’s three personas have different needs from structured stablecoin data:

FinTech / Compliance professional
Needs regulatory status, attestation links, chain support — all on one page, citable. Entity pages deliver this.
Remittance-oriented user
Needs to understand what a specific stablecoin is and whether it’s safe before using it in a playbook. Entity pages deliver this.
Curious learner
Lands on a search result for “what is USDC” or “is PYUSD regulated” and needs a clear, structured answer. Entity pages deliver this.

The comparison tool primarily serves the first persona. Entity pages serve all three and also generate the organic traffic that builds the audience for everything else.

1.4  Entity Pages are the highest-ROI SEO investment available
High-volume, well-answerable search queries currently unaddressed:
	•	"USDC reserve attestation" — high intent, poorly served
	•	"is USDT regulated in Singapore" — corridor-specific, high trust value
	•	"EURC vs USDC" — will surface entity pages even before the comparison tool launches
	•	"PYUSD backing mechanism" — growing query, thin results

Each entity page is a long-term organic traffic asset. The Comparison Tool is a useful tool but is unlikely to rank as well as properly structured entity pages for informational queries.

2.  Product Overview
2.1  What this feature is
A structured directory of stablecoins, each with a dedicated page containing: identity metadata, reserve and attestation data, regulatory alignment by jurisdiction, chain/network support, market context, and a timeline of notable events. The directory is browsable, filterable, and individually linkable. Every stablecoin the Atlas references in news, playbooks, or careers can link to its entity page.

2.2  What it is not
	•	Not a trading terminal. No buy/sell actions.
	•	Not financial advice. All reserve and market data is informational and disclaimed.
	•	Not a wallet or custodian. No fund-holding functionality.
	•	Not a comparison tool (Phase 3). This phase builds the data layer; the comparison UI comes next.

3.  User Stories
3.1  FinTech / Compliance professional
	•	As a compliance officer at a bank, I want to find the regulatory alignment status of USDC in Singapore in under 30 seconds, so I can cite it in an internal briefing without hunting across multiple sources.
	•	As a product manager at a fintech, I want to see all chains that USDC is supported on, so I know which networks are relevant for our integration.
	•	As a risk analyst, I want to access the most recent reserve attestation report for USDT, so I can assess backing risk without having to navigate Tether’s site.

3.2  Remittance-oriented user
	•	As someone in Singapore sending money to the UK, I want to understand whether the stablecoin recommended in the playbook is safe and backed before I use it, so I feel confident putting real money on it.
	•	As a first-time stablecoin user, I want a plain-English summary of what “reserve-backed” means for the specific coin I’m about to use, not a generic definition.

3.3  Curious learner
	•	As someone who Googled “what is PYUSD”, I want to land on a page that tells me who issued it, what backs it, whether it’s regulated, and where I can use it — all without needing to understand DeFi.
	•	As a reader of the Stablecoin Atlas newsletter, I want to click through from the newsletter to the attestation page the issue referenced, and actually find it.

5.  Page Structure: /stablecoins/[slug]
Each entity page is divided into six zones. The order is deliberate: the most-searched and trust-critical information (identity, reserve backing) appears first, before market and event data.

Zone 1 — Identity Header (above the fold)
Stablecoin name + ticker
e.g. USD Coin (USDC)
Issuer
Linked to issuer entity page (Companies section)
Peg currency
USD / EUR / GBP / SGD
Type
Fiat-backed / Algorithmic / Commodity-backed / CDP
One-line plain-English summary
Max 2 sentences. No jargon. Answers: what is it, who made it, what backs it.
Trust badges
REGULATED_FRAMEWORK_ALIGNED / REDEMPTION_POLICY_DISCLOSED / ATTESTATION_OR_AUDIT_LINKED (see badge spec in Phase 1 PRD)
Last verified date
When Atlas last reviewed/updated this page

Zone 2 — Reserve & Attestation Panel

NOTE:  This is the panel promised in the newsletter. It is the highest-trust zone on the page. Every field must have a source link or be explicitly marked “not disclosed.”

Backing mechanism
Plain-English description (e.g. “Held in cash and short-term US Treasuries at regulated financial institutions”)
Reserve composition
Asset breakdown if disclosed (e.g. 80% US Treasuries, 20% cash). Source link required.
Attestation frequency
Monthly / Quarterly / Annual / Not disclosed
Most recent attestation
Date + link to primary source (issuer site or auditor report). If unavailable: “Not publicly available as of [date].”
Attestation provider
Name of auditing/attestation firm
Redemption policy
Who can redeem, minimum amounts, process. Source link. If not disclosed: stated explicitly.
Depeg history
Timeline of any deviation >0.5% from peg, with date and approximate magnitude. Empty state: “No depeg events recorded in Atlas data.”

Zone 3 — Regulatory Alignment
A jurisdiction-by-jurisdiction view of the stablecoin’s regulatory status. Render as a card grid, one card per relevant jurisdiction.

Jurisdiction
Country or region (SG, US, UK, EU, HK, etc.)
Framework
Name of relevant framework (e.g. MAS Stablecoin Framework, MiCA EMT)
Status
Aligned / Under review / Not applicable / Unknown
Basis
One sentence explaining why the classification was made, with evidence link
Last updated
Date of last status review by Atlas editorial

Zone 4 — Chain & Network Support
Supported chains
List of blockchains (Ethereum, Solana, Polygon, etc.) with contract address links to block explorer
Bridge support
Whether cross-chain bridging is officially supported by the issuer, and which bridges are endorsed
Caution note
Inline warning: “Always verify the contract address before sending. Using an unofficial contract can result in permanent loss of funds.”

Zone 5 — Market Context (informational only)

IMPORTANT:  This section is clearly labelled as informational/third-party data. It does not constitute investment advice. Source attribution (e.g. CoinGecko, DeFiLlama) is shown next to each figure.

Market cap
Current USD value, sourced from CoinGecko API
30-day supply change
Mint/burn signal (% change in circulating supply), sourced from DeFiLlama
Chain distribution
% of supply on each chain (DeFiLlama)
24h volume
Approximate trading volume, sourced from CoinGecko

Zone 6 — Notable Events (Timeline)
A reverse-chronological list of tangible actions related to this stablecoin, pulled from the Tangible Tracker data model. Each entry shows: date, action type, one-line summary, evidence link. Examples: chain expansion, new jurisdiction license, reserve attestation published, enforcement action, redemption policy change.

6.  Directory Page: /stablecoins
The directory is the entry point for browse-first users and the SEO-facing landing page for queries like “list of regulated stablecoins” or “SGD stablecoin.”

6.1  Layout
	•	Card grid (default): one card per stablecoin showing name, issuer, peg, trust badges, and market cap.
	•	Table view (toggle): same data in a scannable table, better for professional users comparing multiple coins at once.
	•	Corridor filter: pre-filter directory by corridor relevance (e.g. “Show stablecoins relevant to SG → UK”).
	•	Peg filter: USD / EUR / GBP / SGD / Other.
	•	Badge filter: show only ATTESTATION_OR_AUDIT_LINKED coins; show only REGULATED_FRAMEWORK_ALIGNED coins.
	•	Search: live filter by name or ticker within the directory.

6.2  Default sort order
Sort by: (1) badge completeness descending, (2) market cap descending. This ensures the most credible and well-evidenced entries appear first without requiring users to understand the badge system.

7.2  Data sourcing strategy
Market data (market cap, supply, volume)
CoinGecko API (free tier). DeFiLlama API for supply and chain distribution. Refresh daily via cron.
Reserve / attestation data
Manual curation by Atlas editorial team. Link to primary source (issuer website or auditor). Trigger for refresh: newsletter item references an attestation, or 30-day staleness threshold.
Regulatory alignment
Manual curation linked to Regulatory Tracker (Phase 1). Same editorial team. Refresh triggered by tracker updates.
Chain support / contract addresses
Manual curation cross-referenced against issuer’s official documentation. Refresh when chain expansion event logged in Tangible Tracker.
Depeg history
Seeded manually for historical events. Future: DeFiLlama peg data + threshold alert (>0.5%) triggers editorial review.

EDITORIAL INTEGRITY NOTE:  Reserve composition and attestation data must link to primary sources (issuer or auditor). Never use secondary aggregator data for these fields without corroboration. If primary source is unavailable, the field displays “Not publicly available as of [date]” rather than an estimate.

10.  Compliance & Disclaimer Requirements

HARD REQUIREMENT:  All disclaimer requirements from the Phase 1 PRD apply to entity pages. The following are entity-page-specific additions.

	•	Market data zone must be prefaced with: “Market data is sourced from third-party providers (CoinGecko, DeFiLlama) and is provided for informational purposes only. It is not an offer, recommendation, or advice to buy, sell, or use any asset.”
	•	Reserve and attestation data must include: “Attestation data links to issuer or auditor primary sources. Stablecoin Atlas does not verify or audit reserve claims independently.”
	•	Regulatory alignment cards must include: “Regulatory status reflects Atlas editorial assessment and may not reflect current legal status. Verify directly with the relevant regulatory body.”
	•	Depeg history section must include: “Historical depeg events do not predict future stability. Past performance does not indicate future behaviour.”
	•	Chain support table must include the caution note about contract address verification (specified in Zone 4, Section 5).

11.  UX & Design Guidance
11.1  Trust hierarchy
The page should communicate trust visually through hierarchy, not through labels. The trust badges in the header tell the story — the content zones substantiate it. Avoid repeating the word “regulated” on the page; let the Regulatory Alignment zone speak for itself.

11.2  Empty and incomplete states
Every field that cannot be populated must show a clear, honest empty state rather than being omitted or hidden. This is a trust feature, not a limitation:
	•	Attestation not available: “No public attestation report has been found for this stablecoin as of [date]. If you have a source, report it below.”
	•	Regulatory status unknown: “Atlas does not currently have sufficient data to assess this jurisdiction. Last reviewed: [date].”
	•	No depeg events: “No depeg events recorded in Atlas data. This does not guarantee future stability.”

11.3  Mobile-first layout guidance
	•	Zone 1 (Identity Header) and Zone 2 (Reserve Panel) must be fully readable on a 390px viewport without horizontal scroll.
	•	Trust badges should render as pill tags, not icons requiring tooltip hover.
	•	Contract addresses in Zone 4 should truncate with copy-to-clipboard, never overflow.
	•	Regulatory alignment cards should stack vertically on mobile (not a horizontal scrolling carousel).

11.4  Cross-links and contextual discovery
	•	From Latest articles: stablecoin mentions auto-link to entity pages.
	•	From Playbooks: stablecoin suggestions link to entity pages with an anchor to the Regulatory Alignment zone (relevant to the corridor jurisdiction).
	•	From entity pages: “Related news” pulls the 3 most recent Latest items tagged to this stablecoin.
	•	From entity pages: “Used in corridors” shows which playbooks include this stablecoin with a direct link.

12.  Content Operations
12.1  Launch content requirements
Each of the 10 MVP stablecoins must have the following fields populated at launch (P0) or within 2 weeks post-launch (P1):

P0 at launch
Name, ticker, issuer, peg, type, summary, trust badges, backing description, latest attestation link or honest empty state, redemption policy link or honest empty state, regulatory alignment for at least 2 of: SG / US / UK / EU, supported chains (top 3 chains minimum)
P1 within 2 weeks
Full reserve composition breakdown, all relevant jurisdiction regulatory cards, depeg history (seeded for known events), chain support complete, notable events timeline populated from Tangible Tracker

12.2  Maintenance model
	•	Attestation data: review triggered when a new attestation is published by the issuer. Target SLA: update within 48 hours of publication.
	•	Regulatory alignment: review triggered by Regulatory Tracker updates. Same editorial team maintains both.
	•	Market data: API-driven, auto-refreshed daily. No manual maintenance required.
	•	Notable events: auto-populated from Tangible Tracker. No separate workflow.
	•	Staleness indicator: if last_verified_at is >60 days, the page shows a subtle banner: “This page is due for review. Data may not reflect the latest developments.”

14.  Out of Scope (this phase)
	•	Comparison Tool (“Compare USDC vs USDT”) — Phase 3, built on this data layer.
	•	Issuer entity pages (Companies section) — separate workstream; entity pages link to them but don’t require them to launch.
	•	Regulator entity pages — separate workstream; regulatory alignment zone links to them but functions without them.
	•	User-submitted data or corrections beyond the “Report outdated info” link.
	•	Price or exchange rate calculators — addressed in Tools section (separate workstream).
	•	Historical supply charts beyond the 30-day snapshot — Phase 3 with expanded data infrastructure.
	•	API endpoint for developer access to entity data — Phase 4.

Appendix: Comparison Tool — Deferred to Phase 3
The comparison tool is not being abandoned — it is being sequenced correctly. Once entity pages are live and the data model is populated, the comparison tool is primarily a UI concern: render two or more entity page data objects side-by-side, highlighting differences.

When Phase 3 is scoped, the comparison tool should address:
	•	URL structure: /compare?coins=usdc,usdt,pyusd (shareable, linkable)
	•	Fields to compare: peg, issuer, backing mechanism, attestation frequency, regulatory alignment by jurisdiction, chains supported, market cap, 30-day supply change
	•	Highlighting: auto-highlight where coins differ (not where they’re the same)
	•	SEO target queries: “USDC vs USDT”, “EURC vs EURS”, “best EUR stablecoin”
	•	Disclaimer: same requirements as entity pages, applied to the comparison surface


PHASE 3 DEPENDENCY:  Comparison tool requires: entity page data model live (this phase), at least 6 months of attestation data to make “atttestation frequency” meaningful, and Regulatory Tracker updated for all relevant jurisdictions (Phase 1 expansion).
